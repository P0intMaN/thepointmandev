Arrays are the first data structure every programmer learns, and the one most take for granted. But beneath the `[]` operator lies a world of cache lines, pointer arithmetic, amortized costs, and elegant algorithmic patterns.

This course starts from how arrays actually live in memory, then builds up every major array technique — two pointers, sliding windows, prefix sums — with the kind of depth that makes hard problems feel obvious.
